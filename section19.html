19. Radio Discipline and Open Mic Cakes

(1) This section establishes the strict operational pastry fines for any volunteer who causes tactical interference, transmission blockages, or verbal protocol failures on any radio communications network operated by or shared with the unit.
(2) **The Endless Comms & Airwave Catch-All:** For the absolute avoidance of doubt, this section features a completely open-ended extract applying to **any and all communication equipment, frequencies, and networks** utilized during unit operations. This explicitly blankets:
(a) Handheld portable transceivers, vehicle-mounted mobile stations, and base command desk setups;
(b) All active operational bands, including VHF, UHF, analogue channels, and secure digital radio talkgroups; and
(c) Inter-agency networks and shared emergency frequencies where the unit is actively cooperating with blue-light services or local authorities.
(3) Communication "fuck-ups" that trigger an immediate and unappealable cake liability under this universal framework include, but are not limited to—
(a) **The Hot Mic / Open Transmission:** Accidentally leaning on, sitting on, or wedging a radio's Push-To-Talk (PTT) button, resulting in an extended open carrier wave that blocks the channel and treats control to local cab chat, private swearing, or off-key singing;
(b) **The Voice Announcement Startle:** Forgetting to turn down a high-power digital radio terminal before entering a quiet control room, causing a deafening automated channel or profile voice notification to blast out across the command desk;
(c) **The CB Slang Infraction:** Using informal, non-standard phrases, Hollywood movie lingo, or outdated CB radio slang (such as *"10-4 Good Buddy"*, *"What's your twenty?"*, or *"Over and Out"*) instead of clear, official unit voice procedure;
(d) **The Call-Sign Amnesia:** Completely forgetting your assigned sector or tactical vehicle call-sign during a live check-in window, causing an awkward silence on the net followed by an informal *"Yeah, it's Louis here..."*; or
(e) **The Echo Chamber Failure:** Standing directly next to another active responder with your speaker volume on maximum while transmitting, creating a massive, ear-piercing feedback loop across the entire operational grid.
(4) An infraction under this section is legally established the moment control room management has to send a text alert via **WhatsApp**, dispatch a formal reminder via **Email**, or cut across the net to state: *"Station transmitting, you have a stuck mic, clear the airwaves."*
(5) Penalties are assessed based on the level of communication disruption and comedic value:
(a) **The Brief Airwave Slip:** For minor protocol errors or a short open mic lasting less than 5 seconds that does not block an active emergency call: One (1) standard supermarket sharing cake; or
(b) **The Premium Airwave Blackout:** For a major open mic incident that ties up the main talkgroup for over 30 seconds, broadcasts private in-vehicle conversations to the entire team, or disrupts a live event deployment: A mandatory minimum penalty of twelve (12) premium bakery doughnuts to appease the deafened controllers.
