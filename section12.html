12. Lost Equipment Cakes

(1) A Responder commits an operational asset protection infraction under this section if they fail to adequately track, secure, or return any piece of operational gear, hardware, or logistical asset utilized during a deployment, training exercise, or public event.
(2) **The Universal Property Catch-All (Open-Ended Extract):** For the absolute avoidance of doubt, the provisions of this section shall apply to the loss, misplacement, or abandonment of **any and all equipment** present at or involved in an operation, completely irrespective of legal ownership. This explicitly includes:
(a) Official kit owned by, or branded to, the response group;
(b) Personal kit brought along by an individual Responder to supplement their own vehicle or deployment capability (including personal tools, torches, or rigging gear); and
(c) Third-party kit belonging to any external entity, category-1 or category-2 emergency responder, or partner agency being actively assisted by the unit (including but not limited to local authorities, ambulance crews, fire service, or police).
(3) Specific equipment-related blunders that trigger immediate cake liability under this universal framework include—
(a) **The Muddy Recovery Omission:** Leaving an auxiliary tow strap, heavy-duty D-shackle, snatch block, or tree-trunk protector lying on the ground at an incident scene or in a damp field after completing a vehicle extraction;
(b) **The High-Vis Dispersal Action:** Misplacing official high-visibility jackets, operational vests, or safety helmets at an event coordination site or leaving them slung over a staging area fence post; or
(c) **The Mobile Terminal Amnesia:** Dropping a handheld VHF/UHF/Digital radio transceiver, leaving it behind at an active logistics desk, or misplacing a charging cradle.
(4) An offence under this section is established the moment a teammate, controller, or external agency member discovers a missing or stranded asset and is forced to send out an alert via **Email** or **WhatsApp** asking: *"Who left this gear behind?"*
(5) The standard penalty structure for a Section 12 violation demands the immediate provision of one (1) standard cake or treat per person present to the collective table. If the lost item requires a dedicated vehicle turnaround, an audit trail update in **Responder Manager**, or a secondary off-road journey to retrieve it, the ticket is upgraded to an **Aggravated Logistical Failure**, requiring premium bakery-fresh doughnuts scaling strictly to exactly one per person present.
