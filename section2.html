2. Driving Cakes

(1) A Responder commits an offence under this section if, while operating any 4x4 response asset, logistics transport vehicle, or utility trailer, they exhibit a notable lapse in mechanical sympathy, off-road driving technique, or basic spatial awareness.
(2) For the purposes of subsection (1), specific driving infractions requiring immediate pastry-based remediation include—
(a) **The Axle-Deep Citation (4x4-BOGGED):** Causing a high-clearance, four-wheel-drive vehicle to become entirely immobilized in terrain easily negotiable by a standard 3-door supermarket hatchback;
(b) **The Forgotten Leverage Clause:** Sinking an asset completely into wet mud or a damp field before remembering to engage low-range gears, diff-locks, or terrain management settings;
(c) **The Trailer Alignment Anomaly:** Attempting to reverse an auxiliary support trailer and causing a structural jackknife, or spending more than five consecutive minutes attempting to back into a standard parking or staging area; or
(d) **The Scenic Detour Malfeasance:** Becoming logistically disoriented ("lost") while navigating a well-marked road or a simple trail route, resulting in delayed delivery of team support or equipment.
(3) Any offence under this section is automatically upgraded to an **Aggravated Driving Blunder** if the driver explicitly stated immediately prior to the infraction words to the effect of: *"Watch this," "It's just a bit of wet grass,"* or *"Trust me, I have a differential lock."*
(4) Where an asset requires a tow strap recovery from a teammate, or external recovery via agricultural machinery, the driver shall face a mandatory penalty escalation under the provisions of the enforcement framework, requiring premium bakery treats scaling strictly to exactly one per person present.
