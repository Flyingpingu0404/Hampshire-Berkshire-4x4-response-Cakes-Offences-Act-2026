8. Return from Leave Cakes

(1) A Responder triggers a statutory culinary levy if they mark themselves unavailable on the standby rota, log off from active availability within the **Responder Manager** control software, or take an extended holiday, sun vacation, or personal sabbatical.
(2) To make amends for leaving their designated regional sectors short-handed—forcing remaining colleagues to cover potential emergency weather call-outs, flood alerts, or public safety event logistics—the returning Responder must pay a "Holiday Tax" upon their next physical return to a team training meeting, briefing, or deployment area.
(3) The strict scale of the Holiday Tax is directly linked to the climate and environmental conditions enjoyed by the Responder while away:
(a) **Standard Leave:** If the Responder went on a standard domestic break or staycation, they must provide one (1) large supermarket-tier cake or premium swiss roll to the break table;
(b) **The Sunshine Aggravation:** If the Responder boasts about their trip or posts photographic evidence in the team **WhatsApp** group showing a beach, a swimming pool, or cocktails while the rest of the unit is on standby in the rain and mud, the penalty automatically escalates to a minimum of twelve (12) premium bakery-fresh doughnuts; or
(c) **The International Sabbatical Clause:** For any continuous absence from the rota exceeding twenty-eight (28) days, a luxury multi-tier bakery selection or premium platter is mandatory to formally reinstate operational goodwill.
(4) Fulfilling this section completely nullifies any operational resentment held by the controllers or logistics coordinators regarding the culprit's absence. Failure to supply the cake within their first two return cycles will result in an automated system flag in **Responder Manager** and an escalation citation under the bacon roll mandate.
