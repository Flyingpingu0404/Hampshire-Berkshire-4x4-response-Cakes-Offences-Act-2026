10. Communications Mishap Cakes

(1) This section establishes the strict operational discipline and technical standards for all voice nets, data links, and messaging channels used by the unit—including analog VHF/UHF frequencies, digital (DMR) radio networks, Zello channels, emails, and WhatsApp groups.
(2) A Responder commits a clear communications infraction under this section if they cause any of the following technical or operational slip-ups:
(a) **The Hot Mic Infraction:** Unintentionally keying open a handheld radio, mobile vehicle terminal, or Zello channel on a **VHF, UHF, or digital radio network**, resulting in the transmission of in-cab singing, heavy breathing, or private conversations across the operational talkgroup;
(b) **The Frequency Alignment Error:** Transmitting on an incorrect **VHF or UHF simplex channel**, selecting the wrong digital talkgroup, or speaking on a local repeater net during a multi-agency operation without authorization, thereby confusing control;
(c) **The Digital Cross-Post Felony:** Accidentally transmitting a purely personal message, meme, or private photo intended for a family member directly into an official deployment **WhatsApp** group or via a team-wide **Email** chain; or
(d) **The Phantom Update Status:** Marking a response vehicle, trailer, or specialist asset with an entirely incorrect status inside the **Responder Manager** control software, leading controllers to look for a vehicle that is actually parked up at the driver's home address.
(3) An offence under this section is declared made out the exact moment a control room operator or team coordinator has to issue a public reprimand or manually override a radio frequency or messaging channel to clear a user error.
(4) The standard penalty for a Section 10 violation is one (1) standard cake or treat per person present. If a "Hot Mic" transmission ties up an active digital or analog channel during an adverse weather deployment, or a rogue email loop causes inbox chaos for the team, it is upgraded to an **Aggravated Communications Crisis**, requiring a minimum delivery of premium bakery-fresh doughnuts scaling strictly to exactly one per person present to appease the hungry, disrupted team.
