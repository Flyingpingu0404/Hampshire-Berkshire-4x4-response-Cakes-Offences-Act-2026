9. Resolved Complaint Cakes

(1) This section establishes the mandatory culinary arbitration framework for resolving minor disputes, administrative grumbles, technical arguments, or official grievances raised between Responders or logged within the **Responder Manager** environment.
(2) A statutory cake liability is formally triggered under this section whenever—
(a) **The Groundless Grievance:** A Responder raises an official complaint or logs a technical dispute with a regional coordinator, and a subsequent review reveals the complaint was entirely unfounded or caused by the complainant's own failure to read operational updates;
(b) **The Technical Stand-Off:** Two Responders engage in a prolonged argument regarding vehicle specifications, mechanical capabilities, gear ratios, navigation software settings, or routing protocols over an active **WhatsApp** channel, and an official ruling or field test definitively proves one party entirely incorrect; or
(c) **The False Accusation:** A Responder publicly blames a teammate for misplacing an auxiliary asset or failing to update a logbook, only for system records to prove the teammate completely innocent.
(3) The individual found to be legally "in the wrong" by a coordinator or by a clear majority vote of the shift shall be designated the Culprit and sentenced to the immediate procurement of a **"Settlement Bake."**
(4) The standard penalty for a Section 9 violation requires the provision of standard cakes or treats scaling strictly to exactly one item per person present to the next team briefing. If the original dispute caused an unnecessary administrative backlog or delayed a team deployment tracker, the penalty automatically escalates to premium bakery-fresh doughnuts scaling strictly to exactly one per person present to fully restore group harmony.
