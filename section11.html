11. Team Integration and Departures

(1) This section establishes the collaborative frameworks for recognizing significant personnel transitions within the unit, ensuring a welcoming environment for incoming volunteers and a supportive network across all regional groups.
(2) **The Initial Onboarding Grace Period:** All incoming trainees, candidates, and newly registered volunteers are granted total culinary immunity during their initial applications, classroom sessions, and early assessments. The unit actively protects new volunteers from any pastry-based levies during this phase.
(3) **The Full Team Integration Milestone:** A celebratory tradition is formally recognized at the point where a new volunteer finishes their core training path and steps into full, permanent alignment with the team’s active operational and social circles.
(a) To mark this milestone, celebrate shedding their trainee status, and officially commemorate their welcome as a permanent fixture of the regional team circle, the integrated Responder is invited to provide a celebratory pastry asset or selection box at their next local training or social gathering, scaling to one per attendee;
(b) This gesture serves as a traditional rite of passage, welcoming the individual into the unit’s long-standing culture of camaraderie, shared shifts, and mutual support.
(4) **The Standing-Down Provision (Leaving):** When an active volunteer formally transitions away from the live rotas or finishes their service cycle with the unit, a "Final Bake Out" is encouraged as an optional, high-respect tradition to celebrate the shared experiences, cold night call-outs, and successful deployments completed alongside their colleagues.
