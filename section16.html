16. GPS / Navigation Mishap Cakes

(1) This section governs navigational compliance, mapping accuracy, and route-following discipline for all field volunteers acting upon dispatch coordinates, event layouts, or transit orders issued by the management team.
(2) **The Multi-Channel Dispatch Framework:** For the purposes of this section, operational instructions, locations, and routing files are recognized as legally delivered whether pushed down via the **Responder Manager** software, broadcasted verbally over **VHF, UHF, or digital radio networks**, dropped as digital map pins inside a **WhatsApp** stream, or distributed as formal documentation via **Email**.
(3) A volunteer triggers an immediate navigational pastry liability if they commit any of the following location-based blunders:
(a) **The Coordinate Transposition Error:** Incorrectly writing down or typing a location format—such as swapping digits in an Ordnance Survey grid reference, mishearing a What3Words string over a noisy radio channel, or putting latitude and longitude numbers in backward on a personal GPS unit;
(b) **The Sat-Nav Over-Reliance Failure:** Punching a standard postcode into a commercial consumer phone app (like Google Maps or Apple Maps) and blindly following it down a generic public route, completely ignoring the specialized off-road tracks or tactical entry gates explicitly detailed in the management briefing pack;
(c) **The Digital Pin Expiration:** Attempting to navigate using an old or out-of-date WhatsApp live location pin dropped by a teammate who has since moved positions, resulting in the volunteer driving to an empty field or an abandoned control area;
(d) **The Duplicate Destination Mix-Up:** Failing to double-check the destination details, causing the volunteer to navigate to an entirely different village, farm, or street that happens to share an identical name with the active operational sector; or
(e) **The Convoy Cul-de-Sac:** Deviating from the pre-vetted management route and leading a trailing line of heavily laden support trucks, trailers, or partner agency vehicles down an impassable green lane, weight-restricted bridge, or tight residential dead end, forcing a chaotic multi-point turnaround.
(4) **The Open-Ended Tracking Catch-All:** For the absolute avoidance of doubt, the provisions of this section apply to any error where a volunteer misinterprets, mishears, or ignores positioning intelligence provided by management, whether occurring during a blue-light training exercise, a live public event grid, or an active adverse weather deployment.
(5) An infraction under this section is legally established the moment management reviews the tracking logs, notices a response asset wandering hopelessly off-course, and is forced to hail the vehicle over the radio net to ask: *"Control to mobile unit, please state your current heading and clarify your destination."*
(6) Penalties are scaled based on the level of logistical disruption caused to the managed operation:
(a) **The Minor Detour:** If the navigational error is corrected quickly via radio correction and causes no delay to the event schedule, the penalty stands at one (1) standard cake or treat per person present; or
(b) **The Aggravated Wandering:** If the mapping flub causes a volunteer to miss a critical check-in window, delays an emergency deployment, or requires management to re-route secondary support assets to fix the coverage gap, the penalty automatically escalates to premium bakery-fresh doughnuts scaling strictly to exactly one per person present.
