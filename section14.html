14. Training Lapses and Deployment Amnesia

(1) This section establishes the culinary consequences for instructional lapses, practical assessment errors, or the sudden, catastrophic onset of "amnesia" regarding established unit protocols while executing live operations.
(2) A Responder triggers an immediate assessment-tier cake obligation if, during an official training exercise or under evaluation, they—
(a) **The Off-Road Stall / Hang-Up:** Fail a practical driving or recovery maneuver by completely high-centering a vehicle on a basic obstacle, stalling out on clear terrain, or misconfiguring a winch line setup; or
(b) **The Net Protocol Flub:** Fail to demonstrate basic proficiency during a mock radio test on the **VHF, UHF, or digital radio networks**, including completely blanking on standard voice procedures.
(3) **The Live Deployment Amnesia Clause (Open-Ended Extract):** The penalties under this section shall aggressively apply to any live emergency deployment, adverse weather call-out, or public event where an operational Responder blatantly ignores, forgets, or bypasses the exact training they have been given. This explicitly covers:
(a) **The Freelance Protocol:** Winging an operation or setting up an unauthorized recovery rig on a live scene while completely disregarding the standard safety steps explicitly practiced during recent training modules;
(b) **The "I Forgot Everything" Syndrome:** Getting onto a live event or dispatch sector and completely forgetting how to check in on the radio talkgroups, manage status updates in the **Responder Manager** software, or log critical equipment usage via **Email**; or
(c) **The Safety Gear Disregard:** Blatantly ignoring basic vehicle safety setups or personal protective equipment (PPE) protocols on-scene, despite having those exact parameters drummed into them during unit briefings.
(4) An offence under subsection (3) is declared absolute the moment a team leader or coordinator has to step in on a live scene or drop a message into the **WhatsApp** coordination group to correct a responder who "should definitely know better."
(5) The mandatory penalty scale for a Section 14 violation requires the immediate delivery of one (1) large supermarket-tier cake to the break table. If a blatant disregard for training on a live event grid causes a logistical delay or requires another unit member to physically intervene, the ticket is upgraded to a **Premium Insurgency Fine**, requiring a minimum of twelve (12) premium bakery doughnuts to soothe the team's frayed nerves.
