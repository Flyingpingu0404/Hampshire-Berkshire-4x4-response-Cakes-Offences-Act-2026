7. Drug and Medicine

(1) This section governs the strict administrative care, logging, and operational management of vehicle medical kits, first aid provisions, trauma packs, and essential responder stamina substances.
(2) A Responder commits an operational infraction under this section if, during a deployment, training weekend, or shift audit, they—
(a) **The Logbook Omission:** Fail to immediately update the inventory or log the usage of any tracked vehicle first aid asset or trauma pack within the **Responder Manager** control software;
(b) **The Expiry Oversight:** Allow a critical medical kit item, eyewash solution, or bandage pack inside a response vehicle or support trailer to slip past its official expiry date without proactively flagging it via **Email** to the logistics coordinators; or
(c) **The Caffeine Depletion Felony:** Consume the absolute final emergency portion of team coffee, tea bags, sugar, or energy drinks kept in the staging area box or vehicle storage locker without immediately arranging a replacement or logging it for restock.
(3) For the strict purposes of this Act, the definition of "Medicine" is legally extended to include any emergency ration of Haribo, chocolate, or high-sugar snacks maintained in gloveboxes for long-duration bad weather call-outs. Consuming a fellow responder's emergency vehicle sugar stash during a multi-hour flood or snow deployment without explicit, timestamped permission via **WhatsApp** constitutes a high-tier cake theft.
(4) The penalty scale for a Section 7 violation mandates the immediate delivery of one (1) standard cake or treat per person present to the next team meeting, escalating to a premium bakery selection scaling strictly to exactly one per person present if a medical pack discrepancy causes a vehicle or trailer to be temporarily marked as non-operational in **Responder Manager**.
