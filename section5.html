5. Sick Cakes

(1) While a Responder is temporarily stood down from operational duties due to genuine medical illness or physical injury, they shall be completely exempt from all operational cake processing and penalty frameworks for the duration of their recovery.
(2) Upon making a full recovery and formally signing back onto active shift status, a statutory **"Return to Health Levy"** is triggered under the following parameters:
(a) If the absence was minor (under 14 days), the returning Responder is expected to provide minor confections to celebrate their return, scaling strictly to exactly one item per person present;
(b) If the absence was extended (exceeding 14 days), the returning Responder must provide high-grade premium treats scaling strictly to exactly one per person present to thank the team members who covered their operational standby shifts.
(3) It is an offence under this section to falsify a recovery or use "Sick Status" to deliberately dodge a pre-existing cake penalty incurred prior to going on leave. Any such evasion will result in the immediate doubling of the original pastry debt upon their return.
(4) If a Responder suffers an injury *while actively performing an operational recovery* (e.g., pulling a teammate out of a ditch), they are entirely exempt from the Return to Health Levy. Instead, the team member who was rescued shall bear the burden of buying the recovery cakes.
