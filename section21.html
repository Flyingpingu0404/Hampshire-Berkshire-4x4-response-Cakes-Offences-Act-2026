21. Arboreal, Boundary, and Ditch Interventions

(1) This section establishes the mandatory pastry liabilities for any responder who, while operating a response truck or towing a unit asset, experiences an unscheduled physical introduction with local flora, land boundaries, or structural topography.
(2) **The Off-Track Interaction Catch-All:** A driver triggers an immediate and unappealable impact-tier pastry fine if their vehicle or trailing asset makes unauthorized contact with, or becomes un-intentionally wedged within, any static rural or urban obstacle. This explicitly covers:
(a) **The Bush and Tree Sidewipe:** Misjudging the width of a green lane, woodland track, or overgrown event boundary, resulting in a loud, scraping interaction with low-hanging tree branches, dense brambles, or thick bushes;
(b) **The Gatepost Squeeze:** Clipping, scraping, or getting structurally hung up on a metal estate gate, wooden farm gatepost, or narrow width-restriction barrier while trying to maneuver a large response truck or trailer through an access point; or
(c) **The Ditch Misjudgment Failure:** Simply misjudging the layout of the land or track edge and driving directly into an open ditch, drainage trench, or sudden drop, leaving the vehicle completely bottomed out and truly stuck.
(3) **The Open-Ended Impact Framework:** For the absolute avoidance of doubt, the provisions of this section apply to any such contact occurring during active severe weather call-outs, public event deployments, or routine field training nights, completely irrespective of whether the asset sustains a physical dent or merely a layer of pride-shattering mud.
(4) An infraction under this section is legally established the moment a loud crunch or scraping sound is heard by a passenger, when the vehicle requires an immediate tow-strap intervention to escape a ditch, or when a photo of the truck buried in a hedge is uploaded to the main team **WhatsApp** channel.
(5) Penalties are scaled based on the nature of the landscape intervention:
(a) **The Green-Lane Polish:** If the contact merely results in light bush scraping or a minor tracking drop that the driver easily powers out of without stopping the convoy: One (1) standard cake or treat per person present to buff out the embarrassment; or
(b) **The Solid Landscape Wedge:** If the vehicle becomes firmly wedged against a gatepost, leaves a wing mirror dangling from a tree branch, or requires a teammate to winch them out because they are truly stuck in a ditch: A mandatory upgrade to premium bakery-fresh doughnuts scaling strictly to exactly one per person present, plus a box of biscuits for the spotting crew who tried to warn them.
