18. Universal Vehicle Care and Engineering Infractions

(1) This section establishes comprehensive culinary liability for any volunteer who fails to properly inspect, secure, configure, or maintain any vehicle, towed asset, auxiliary system, or engineering component utilized during or directly following unit operations.
(2) **The Endless Engineering & Care Catch-All:** For the absolute avoidance of doubt, the provisions of this section feature a completely open-ended extract. They apply to **any and all vehicular or mechanical failures** caused by oversight, forgetfulness, or incorrect configuration, entirely irrespective of legal ownership. This explicitly blankets:
(a) Official response vehicles, pooled group machinery, and command assets;
(b) Shared auxiliary support trailers, equipment boxes, catering platforms, or generator rigs;
(c) Personal vehicles or private off-roaders brought along by individual volunteers to support a team tasking; and
(d) Third-party or partner agency vehicles actively being assisted, guided, or handled by the unit.
(3) Specific care and maintenance oversights that trigger immediate cake liability under this universal framework include, but are are not limited to—
(a) **The Auxiliary Power Drain:** Leaving primary switches, high-draw light bars, work lights, or radio terminals active when the engine is off, resulting in a dead starter or flat auxiliary battery that grounds the asset;
(b) **The Shore-Line Snatch:** Attempting to drive any vehicle, mobile control unit, or trailer forward while it remains physically plugged into a mains power charging cable, shore-line socket, or workshop hook-up;
(c) **The Unsecured External Component:** Forgetting to lock down a trailer hitch coupler, failing to snap shut bonnet latches, leaving locker doors unlatched, or driving off with tools, oil bottles, or gear left loose sitting inside the engine bay or on a bumper step;
(d) **The Auxiliary System Misconfiguration:** Improperly isolating, wiring, or managing off-grid electrical setups—including solar panels, MPPT charge controllers, split-charging relays, or inverter switches—causing a system lockout or flat auxiliary banks;
(e) **The Critical Fluid or Fuel Omission:** Presenting a vehicle or generator for an active deployment with fuel, engine oil, coolant, or basic fluids well below safe operating levels, forcing an emergency top-up on the grid; or
(f) **The General Mechanical Oversight:** Any unlisted care or maintenance lapse that results in an avoidable technical delay, a breakdown in the compound, or a required intervention by a teammate.
(4) An infraction under this section is legally established the moment a teammate has to break out jump leads, disconnect a trailing cable, secure a loose latch, or flag a maintenance delay via **WhatsApp**, **Email**, or **Responder Manager**.
(5) Penalties are scaled based on the level of disruption and the amount of peer banter generated:
(a) **The Minor Yard Oversight:** For basic care lapses caught and rectified in the compound without affecting response status: One (1) standard cake or treat per person present; or
(b) **The Grounded Response Aggravation:** If a flat battery, improper trailer hook-up, system lockout, or dry fuel tank delays a live deployment, disrupts a public event, or requires a teammate to perform emergency field engineering: A mandatory minimum penalty of premium bakery-fresh doughnuts scaling strictly to exactly one per person present.
