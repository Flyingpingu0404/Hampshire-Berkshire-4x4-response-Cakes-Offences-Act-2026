## PART 1: PRELIMINARY

### 1. Short title and commencement
* **(1)** This Act may be cited as the Hampshire & Berkshire 4x4 Response Cakes (Offences) Act 2026.
* **(2)** This Act shall come into force immediately upon the occurrence of any blunder triggering collective facepalming by a majority of the shift[cite: 2].

### 2. Interpretation and definitions
* **(1)** In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—
  * `all-terrain asset` means any four-wheel-drive (4x4) response vehicle, utility truck, or vehicle engineered to master harsh landscapes[cite: 2];
  * `the Court` means the collective body of immediate peers, colleagues, or tea-room occupants present during the shift[cite: 2];
  * `the Culprit` means the team member identified as responsible for the triggering incident[cite: 2];
  * `minor error` means a mistake fixable within five minutes that does not cause external laughter or operational recovery protocols[cite: 2].

## PART 2: OPERATIONAL AND VEHICULAR OFFENCES

### 3. Improper grounding of an all-terrain vehicle (4x4-BOGGED)
* **(1)** A person commits an offence under this section if, while operating a multi-thousand-pound, high-clearance, all-terrain response asset, they cause the vehicle to become immobilized, stuck, or structurally wedged[cite: 2].
* **(2)** An offence under subsection (1) is fully made out if the terrain causing the immobilization could have been successfully navigated by any of the following alternate methods—
  * **(a)** a standard front-wheel-drive supermarket hatchback[cite: 2];
  * **(b)** an ordinary pedestrian wearing basic footwear[cite: 2]; or
  * **(c)** a light breeze moving across a gravel driveway[cite: 2].

### 4. Aggravated grounding factors
* **(1)** An offence under section 3 shall be escalated to an “Aggravated Cake Offence” if any of the following secondary factors are established by radio traffic or photographic evidence—
  * **(a)** the culprit explicitly claimed, immediately prior to the grounding, words to the effect of: *“Watch this,” “Don't worry, the truck can handle it,”* or *“It’s just a little bit of wet grass”*;
  * **(b)** the culprit failed to engage the central differential lock, low-range gearbox, or appropriate off-road terrain settings until the vehicle was already resting completely on its axles[cite: 2]; or
  * **(c)** the extrication of the vehicle required the deployment of a secondary response vehicle, a heavy kinetic recovery tow rope, or a local farmer operating agricultural machinery[cite: 2].

### 5. General acts of team embarrassment
* **(1)** A person commits an offence if they carry out an action that compromises the professional or intellectual standing of the shift[cite: 2].
* **(2)** Prima facie evidence of an offence under this section includes—
  * **(a)** dropping, spilling, or otherwise destroying a fresh tray of hot beverages intended for the responder team;
  * **(b)** locking the keys inside an operational vehicle or base while fully visible through the glass pane[cite: 2]; or
  * **(c)** utilizing the “Reply All” email function to transmit a personal message, acknowledgment, or basic salutation to an audience exceeding fifty (50) personnel[cite: 2].

## PART 3: ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTY SCALES

### 6. The Fixed Cake Penalty notice
* **(1)** Where an authorized peer observes an offence under Part 2, they may issue a Fixed Cake Penalty notice[cite: 2].
* **(2)** The penalty assigned shall scale dynamically in proportion to the severity of the blunder—
  * **(a)** *for a standard operational error:* one (1) large, chocolate-faced Caterpillar-style cake or two (2) multi-packs of branded store bakery goods[cite: 2];
  * **(b)** *for an aggravated offence:* a minimum of twelve (12) gourmet or premium bakery-fresh doughnuts[cite: 2];
  * **(c)** *for a farmer-assisted extraction:* a luxury multi-tier bakery platter or a verified home-baked sponge featuring genuine double cream[cite: 2].

### 7. Timeframes for culinary compliance
* **(1)** The culprit must deliver the designated legal tender to the main break room tea table within seven (7) calendar days of the initial ticket issuance[cite: 2].
* **(2)** During the grace period specified in subsection (1), the culprit shall be legally immune from further cake processing for the *same* specific incident, but remains fully liable to lighthearted office banter and custom computer desktop backgrounds[cite: 2].

### 8. Escalation to the Bacon Mandate
* **(1)** Failure to fulfill the penalty terms within seven (7) days shall constitute a severe contempt of the Court[cite: 2].
* **(2)** Upon default, the fine automatically converts to a **Mandatory Hot Breakfast Roll Levy**, requiring the delivery of hot bacon, sausage, or egg rolls for the entire immediate shift at the culprit's sole expense[cite: 2].

## SCHEDULE 1: THE STATUTORY DEFINITION OF CAKE

### 1. Lawful culinary tender
The following items are recognized as legal tender for the clearing of a cake fine under this Act—
* **(a)** premium Swiss rolls or large iced novelty caterpillars[cite: 2];
* **(b)** fresh assortments from an artisan or high-street commercial bakery[cite: 2]; and
* **(c)** full-sized family sharing cakes of sponge, fudge, or fruit variety[cite: 2].

### 2. Illegal substitutions and exclusions
* **(1)** For the absolute avoidance of doubt, **Jaffa Cakes do not constitute cake** for the purposes of discharging a standard or aggravated offence under this Act[cite: 2].
* **(2)** Notwithstanding subsection (1), Jaffa Cakes may be tendered as valid payment *only* if the underlying infraction is deemed a minor tech error (e.g., locking oneself out of a computer terminal)[cite: 2].
* **(3)** Multipacks of everyday lunchbox snack bars, half-eaten biscuit assortments, or healthy alternative oat bars are strictly prohibited[cite: 2]. The tendering of such items shall be viewed as an intentional insult to the Court and may result in an immediate doubling of the penalty phase[cite: 2].
