3. Improper Grounding of an All-Terrain Asset (4x4-BOGGED)

(1) A Responder is guilty of an offence if, while operating a high-clearance, four-wheel-drive response vehicle, utility truck, or trailer configuration, they cause said asset to become entirely immobilized or stuck.
(2) An offence under subsection (1) is fully made out if the terrain causing the immobilization could have been successfully navigated by:
(a) A standard front-wheel-drive 1.0L delivery hatchback;
(b) A civilian pedestrian navigating with an ordinary shopping cart; or
(c) A mild puddle measuring less than two inches in depth.
(3) An offence under this section is automatically classified as an Aggravated Grounding if:
(a) The driver explicitly claimed right before the incident: "Don't worry, I know this track," or "It's just a tiny bit of wet grass";
(b) The driver completely forgot to engage low-range settings or center diff-locks until the vehicle was already buried to its axles; or
(c) Extraction required a recovery tow strap from a teammate, or mechanical intervention by a local tractor.
